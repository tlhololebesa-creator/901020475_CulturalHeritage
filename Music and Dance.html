Music and Dance

Dancing is a form of one expressing themself, so it is of no surprice that the Basotho peoplehave their own way of expressing themselves through music and dance, which not only do they sing, but also use wonderful musical instruments to make the songs sound even better.

There are different dances and music in Lesotho, which one could enjoy. For example:we have different dances such as mohobelo,mokhibo,litolobonya and music genre like Famo.


Litolobonya


This is a traditional dance done by females moving to the rhythm of the song, expecially from the waist downward.

Mokhibo


This is a traditional dance done by females, which involves kneeling, sholder movement and some singing.

Lesiba


Lesiba is a mouth musical instrument made from a stick, a horse-tail hair string and a bird's quill. It is played by herd boys for entertainment.

Mamokhorong


This is a single string violin used for musical entertainment.

Moropa


This is a drum used when there are performances, or people are singing.

Sekupu


This a double headed drum, with a skin head on each end.

Setolotolo


This is a simple string instrument play by using ones' hands and mouth.

Thomo


Thomo is a musical instrument that has a calabash attached to a bow made of mountain bamboo near the bottom end, and string made of horse-tail string.

Koriana


This an instrument used used for Sesotho music, expecially in Famo music.

Mohobelo


This is a traditional men's dance which involves a lot of high jumping and stomping.

Ndlamo


Gum Boot Dance

A dance that reflects the influence of migrant labor on Basotho culture.

Famo

A style of music that features the accordion and and an oil-can drum, often accompanied by dancing.

Communal Dancing

Children learn to imitate dancing from community events and ritual like the "Lebollo initiation rites".

Lekolulo

A type of flute played by herding boys to mimic sounds of nature.

Oil-Can Drum

Another instrument central to the Famo music and dance

Pito

A whistle held in the mouth.

Mokhope

A bowed with a thin dry stick.

Sekebeku(Jaw Harp)

It is a mouth instrument piece.

Lekope

It is a mouth instrument made of bamboo, and it is mostly played by the elderly.

Setinkane

A thumb piano, which adds mesmerizing tone Basotho musical composition.

Pina Tsa Mokopu

Songs sung by young girls as a way of entertainment and as a game.

Pina Tsa Selia-lia

Songs sang during a game played by young boys and girls, and they dance to the rhythm of songs.
