Thethana


Thethana is a an attire worn by the young Basotho females. It has been worn for more than two centuries. The skirt is made from woven fibres, such as tsikitlane grass, and is decorated with glass seed beads. Although with the morden days, the thethana is not worn as often as the olden days, but, it is still part of the Basotho culture which is still relevent till today.
To find out more about the thethana please the link below. https://smarthistory.org/thethana-south-sotho-artist/

Makoko


Makoko are usually the attire made of cow skin. It can either be worn by males or females and they have different names called by the two different genders.

Traditional Shoes


This are shoes made of leather, with laces, and traditionally worn by Basotho. To make this type of shoe, a cowhide was scraped, dried, softened and then cut into the desired shape and shoe size.

Seshoeshoe Dresses


The seshoeshoe dresses is a morden way of expressing the Basotho culture , and a way to secure cultural values of the elder Basotho.

Tshea


This is a traditional attire worn by younger males.

Tsoape


This is an attire worn by younger male, during their initiation ceremony.

Traditional Hats


The Basotho hats are one of the most extra ordinarilly creative hats, which one could come across. They come in different types, sizes and colours.

Blankets


Worn by the people of the Mountain Kingdom of Lesotho in Southern Africa, Basotho tribal blankets have a storied history and are worn for both ceremonial and everyday use as well as being used as cozy throws and bedding. Basotho blankets are sold all over the world.
